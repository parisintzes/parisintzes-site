001/006

Direction

Experience is not designed as a visual outcome, but as a perceptual system.

The work investigates how multisensory stimuli shape behavior, emotion, and cognitive response across hospitality environments. It is concerned with the way atmospheres are absorbed, interpreted, and translated into meaningful experience.

002/006

Contexts

Hospitality is approached as a dynamic system of perception, space, and embodied interaction.

The research spans across gastronomy, tourism environments, and multisensory design settings where atmosphere becomes a behavioral condition rather than a decorative layer.

003/006

Method

The methodology integrates sensory science, behavioral interpretation, and analytical structure.

By combining conceptual framing with empirical inquiry, the work translates perception into research evidence and research evidence into experiential intelligence.

004/006

Work & Studies

Selected work explores multisensory experience, eating behavior, and environmental meaning.

The field includes Epignosis Study 1, Epignosis Study 2, and research experience connected to sensory science and hospitality contexts, forming a continuous trajectory between theory, measurement, and application.

005/006

Profile

Paris Intzes is a Hospitality Experience Strategist and Researcher working at the intersection of multisensory experience, eating behavior, and experiential design.

With a background in food science, gastronomy, tourism management, and marketing analytics, his work integrates scientific inquiry with applied experience composition across hospitality and sensory environments.
